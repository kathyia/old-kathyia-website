Working Agreements

About

Working agreements (AKA team norms) are guidelines developed by a team for how they will work together to achieve their objectives. Working agreements describe positive behaviors that, although basic, can vary from group to group or be forgotten in the moment. For example, an agreement might be “We all agree to participate fully.”
Agreements are one of the ways a team 'sharpens the saw.' The purpose of setting up team norms is to establish a one unifying team culture, and it is something to refer back when things get rocky.

How to facilitate

Goal

Establish a team working agreement to describe how we will interact with one another and plan, set, and achieve common goals.

Agenda

Reserve at least one hour for discussion & decision. Here is starting point you can build off of:
  1. Give a short introduction to the goal (see videos below) & distribute materials such as index cards
  2. Pair off for brainstorming, 2-3 ideas per pair
  3. Come back together to compile ideas as a group
  4. Vote & agree on items for the team working agreement

Things to consider

  1. How do task/code reviews work?
  2. How do we get feedback from stakeholders?
  3. Core working hours?
  4. How do we share Discovery work and talk about prototypes?
  5. How do we communicate non-squad obligations?
  6. How do we work with stakeholders?
  7. Expected behavior for meetings (what does "on-time" mean, do we bring laptops or not, etc.)
  8. How do we handle prod issues?
