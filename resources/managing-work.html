Managing Work

Techniques

The To-Do List

Ah, the tried and true To-Do list. Why does it appear in this page about managing work? Because it is foundational to work processes no matter how sophisticated you get. Even if you are an advanced scrum/agile team using Jira to manage work projects across time zones, you will probably still use lists as you think about how to attack a story or how technical implementation details answer the user’s goal. And if you are a solitary startup founder, this might be all you need to start out. Ultimately, it’s worth understanding how to do this foundational piece well because you will understand how To-Do lists compare to other methods such as Kanban and chunking.

A great book I’d recommend is The Checklist Manifesto by Atul Gawande.

Kanban

Key principles:

  1. "Visualize work: Make work visible for better management as well as to identify queues and bottlenecks (or personal procrastination on specific tasks).
  2. Limit Work in Progress (WIP): Increase focus and set priorities, manage overload (keep enough margin). With the right amount of work in progress, the team can be in the flow instead of facing anxiety or boredom.
  3. Focus on the Flow: Analyze the workflow to increase productivity and constantly try to improve the smoothness of the flow.
  4. Continuous Improvement: Continuously improve the effectiveness of the team with focus on the flow and improving your system."

~ Source AgileLeanLife.com

Benefits

  • Quick, low maintenance
  • Easy to maintain across web + mobile
  • Good for scaling up to a (small) focused team

For more on setting up a Kanban system, see this guide to using Trello.com for Kanaban

Work Chunking & the Rapid Planning Method

Let’s call multi-tasking out for what it is, shall we? The pressure will always be there to take on too many things at once. I like the concept behind chunking & RPM for how it applies discipline to looking at what we really should focus on to accomplish more.

I like these two articles about the method and how to get started: The Balance Careers & Tony Robbins’ The Power of Chunking
